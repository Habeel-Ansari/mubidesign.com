Horizon Analytics

Redesigning the core dashboard experience for an AI-powered analytics platform — making complex data feel obvious for enterprise teams.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

6 months (2024)

Team

1 Designer, 4 Engineers, 1 PM

Tools

Figma, Maze, Notion

Overview

The challenge

Horizon Analytics had built a powerful AI engine that surfaced meaningful insights from complex data — but the interface made it nearly impossible to extract value quickly. Enterprise users were spending 45+ minutes per session to find the answers they needed.

My goal was to redesign the core dashboard experience from the ground up: reduce time-to-insight, make AI outputs more legible, and create a scalable design system that could support rapid feature development.

Discovery

Understanding the problem space

Before touching any design tools, I spent 3 weeks in deep research. I ran 18 user interviews across 6 enterprise accounts, shadowed power users in live sessions, and analysed support ticket patterns to identify the highest-friction moments.

Three core themes emerged: users couldn't quickly distinguish AI-generated insights from raw data; the navigation model forced too many context switches; and the information hierarchy didn't match how analysts actually thought about their work.

Process

How I approached the redesign

01

Jobs-to-be-done mapping

Translated research findings into 8 primary user jobs. Prioritised based on frequency and pain intensity. This became the north star for every design decision.

02

Information architecture overhaul

Ran card sorting sessions with 12 users to understand their mental models. Rebuilt the navigation from scratch around insight categories rather than data source types.

03

Design system foundation

Created a token-based design system with components for data tables, chart types, and AI insight cards — all accessible to WCAG AA standard.

04

Iterative testing & refinement

Ran 4 rounds of usability testing with 6 users each. Iterated on the insight card hierarchy, filter patterns, and AI explainability features based on direct observation.

Solution

What we shipped

The redesigned dashboard centres on three core improvements: an AI Insight Feed that surfaces the most relevant findings automatically; a redesigned navigation that reduces context switching by 70%; and a Chart Canvas that makes complex data relationships immediately scannable.

The new design system (128 components, fully documented) enabled the engineering team to ship follow-on features 40% faster and with dramatically fewer design QA rounds.

Reflection

What I learned

The biggest lesson from this project was the importance of making AI outputs legible. Users didn't distrust the AI — they were confused about what they were supposed to do with its outputs. Labelling, context, and clear calls-to-action around AI insights made a bigger difference than any visual changes.

I also learned the compounding value of investing in design systems early. The 6 weeks spent building a solid foundation paid back within the first quarter, enabling features that would have taken months in the old setup.
