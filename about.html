I'm a Senior Product Designer with 9 years of experience across B2B SaaS, fintech, maritime intelligence, and early-stage startups. I don't just design interfaces - I help teams figure out what to build, why it matters, and how to ship it without losing coherence at scale.

I'm probably more energized thinking about token architecture and AI workflow infrastructure than about any individual interface - which is why the teams I work with tend to move faster after I leave than before I arrived.

Designing with AI

I didn't just adopt AI tools. I redesigned how design teams work.

Over the past few years, I've actively integrated AI into the design workflow - not as a novelty, but as a structural shift in how teams operate. The result: faster research synthesis, better documentation, fewer design-to-dev bugs, and more time for the thinking that actually matters.

Research synthesis
Used AI to synthesise interview transcripts, support tickets, and usage data into structured insight maps - replacing two-day synthesis workshops with focused two-hour sessions.
~75% faster synthesis cycles
Spec & documentation
Introduced AI-assisted design spec generation - converting annotated Figma frames into structured component documentation that engineers could act on directly. Cut back-and-forth by half.
~50% reduction in design QA rounds
Design exploration
Used AI to rapidly generate layout alternatives, naming conventions, microcopy variations, and component states - compressing exploration phases that previously took days into hours.
3–5× more concepts explored per sprint
Stakeholder communication
Leveraged AI to draft product briefs, decision memos, and design rationale documents - giving stakeholders better context and reducing the number of alignment meetings needed before development begins.
Fewer pre-dev alignment meetings
Prototyping & testing
Used AI-assisted prototyping to generate realistic data states, edge case variations, and interaction logic documentation - making usability tests more realistic and developer handoffs cleaner.
Richer test scenarios, fewer post-launch bugs
Team capability building
Introduced AI tooling to junior designers on the team - structured prompt frameworks, workflow templates, and review checklists - compressing the gap between junior and mid-level output quality.
Faster junior-to-mid progression

How I work

A few things that have stayed constant across nine years and every kind of team.

01

Business context before pixels

I spend the first part of every project understanding the business model, the competitive landscape, and the constraints. Design that ignores those things ships and fails.

02

Systems, not screens

One-off screens solve one problem. Systems solve categories of problems and get faster to extend over time. I default to thinking in patterns before I think in components.

03

Engineering is a design partner

I write thorough specs, flag implementation challenges before they become build problems, and treat engineers as co-designers - not executors. The best products come from that partnership.

04

Fewer decisions, made well

Great product design is mostly about what you choose not to include. I push teams to make deliberate tradeoffs rather than accumulate features until nothing feels intentional.

05

Ship, learn, iterate

Shipping imperfect work and learning from real users is almost always better than polishing indefinitely. Momentum builds better products than perfection does.

06

Lead by raising the floor

Strong design teams aren't built by protecting high standards from the top - they're built by raising the baseline. Mentoring, clear standards, and shared ownership compound faster than individual excellence.
