The problem

Manual bookings were costing more than the bookings were worth

Green Getaways runs eco-friendly stays around Bangalore. Camping, farm stays, day outings. The product was solid, but the booking process behind it wasn't. Every enquiry came in through WhatsApp or a contact form, got checked against a shared spreadsheet, confirmed by hand, then chased for payment. When two of us checked the same spreadsheet at once, we'd end up with a double-booking. It happened more than I'd like to admit.

On top of the errors, it was eating 3-4 hours a day that should've gone into actually looking after guests.

Approach

React for the booking experience, WordPress underneath

The plan was simple enough: keep WordPress as the backend and admin layer, since that's where we already worked, and build a custom React frontend for the actual booking flow, where speed and real-time availability mattered most. Off-the-shelf booking plugins all assumed hotel-style fixed rooms, which didn't fit. We needed per-property capacity, flexible rates, and blocked dates for maintenance.

The core pieces: a property structure in WordPress with per-property availability, a React booking interface that checks availability live as guests pick dates, and Razorpay handling payment inline. Confirmations, reminders, and receipts fire automatically when a booking's status changes. Built end-to-end with Claude Code over two weeks.

Design & build process

Designing the booking flow end to end

01

Booking flow UX

Designed the guest journey from browse to availability check to booking to payment to confirmation, with real-time availability shown as dates are selected, not after the form is submitted. That one change killed the "went through the whole flow only to find it's unavailable" drop-off we kept seeing before.

02

React booking interface

Built the booking frontend in React, talking to a WordPress backend that manages per-property availability and booking state: pending, confirmed, cancelled. Availability lives server-side now, not in a spreadsheet that can fall out of sync.

03

WordPress admin dashboard

A monthly calendar across all properties, colour-coded by status, with one-click date blocking for maintenance. No spreadsheets, no manual cross-checking.

04

Automated emails

Instant confirmation, payment receipt, a 7-day pre-arrival reminder, and a post-stay follow-up, all triggered automatically and written to feel personal rather than templated.

Key decisions

What made the difference

Showing availability live, as guests pick dates, was the single biggest improvement over the old contact-form flow. It cut false-start drop-off and made the booking feel trustworthy right from the start.

Building custom rather than configuring an off-the-shelf plugin took longer upfront, but every existing option assumed hotel-style room logic that just didn't match our actual capacity and rate structure. Going custom meant the data model could match reality instead of fighting it.

I built the admin side assuming whoever ran it day-to-day wouldn't be technical. Every action takes two clicks or fewer from the calendar view.

Outcome

What changed after launch

Double-bookings went to zero immediately. The 3-4 hours of daily admin work, the spreadsheet checks, the manual WhatsApp confirmations, the manually written emails, dropped to near-zero. That freed us up to focus on the guest experience instead of the back-office.

Guests who previously waited up to 24 hours for confirmation now get it instantly, and that noticeably improved conversion with guests who'd previously found the wait frustrating enough to book elsewhere.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

The Razorpay integration brought more complexity than I expected, particularly around partial payments, cancellations, and refund flows. Next time I'd map those edge cases out before building, instead of handling them as they came up.

I'd also prioritize a guest account feature sooner. Returning guests currently have to re-enter their details every time, and a lightweight account system would unlock both a better repeat-booking experience and real occupancy data for us.
